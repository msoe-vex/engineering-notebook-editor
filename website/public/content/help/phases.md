# The Design Process

Following a structured design process is a core requirement for a high-scoring engineering notebook.

## 1. Define Problem
**What to include:**
- Problem/Design statement
- SMART Goals (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound)
- Criteria & Constraints (What it must do, what limits exist)
- Deliverables and Deadlines

> **Expert Tip:** Be as specific as possible. If you don't define the problem clearly, your solution might solve the wrong thing.

## 2. Generate Concepts
**What to include:**
- Research & Citations (What have others done?)
- Brainstorming sessions
- Sketches and CAD concepts
- Decision Matrix (Comparing ideas based on criteria)

> **Expert Tip:** Quantity over quality initially. Use a decision matrix to objectively choose the best path forward.

## 3. Develop Solution
**What to include:**
- Detailed CAD & Sketches with dimensions
- Math calculations and physics justifications
- Pseudocode for algorithms
- Clear annotations explaining design choices

> **Expert Tip:** This is your blueprint. Someone should be able to build your design just by looking at this section.

## 4. Construct & Test
**What to include:**
- Build pictures with annotations
- Parts list and instructions
- Test Plan (How will you measure success?)
- Quantitative Data (Tables, Charts, Graphs)
- Analysis of results

> **Expert Tip:** Document your failures just as much as your successes. Data is the core of a good notebook.

## 5. Evaluate Solution
**What to include:**
- Reflection on Criteria/Constraints
- Recommended initial improvements
- Event reflections and feedback
- Final conclusion

> **Expert Tip:** Be honest. What would you do differently if you had another week?
